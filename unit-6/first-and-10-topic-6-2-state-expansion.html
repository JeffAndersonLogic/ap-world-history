King Leopold II and the Belgian Congo

A map can show imperial borders, but it cannot show the process that produced them. Between 1750 and 1900, states used treaties, chartered companies, settlers, armies, and administrative takeovers to turn influence into rule.

British and French rule in West Africa

In the Congo, Leopold II first claimed a private domain through diplomacy and a humanitarian façade. International outrage later pushed the Belgian government to replace personal rule with formal colonial administration. Elsewhere, companies such as the Dutch East India Company gave way to direct state control.

Japanese expansion into East Asia

Comparison matters: conquest in Africa, settler expansion in the Pacific, and contiguous growth by Russia, Japan, and the United States all shifted sovereignty, but they did so through different combinations of force, migration, law, and diplomacy.
