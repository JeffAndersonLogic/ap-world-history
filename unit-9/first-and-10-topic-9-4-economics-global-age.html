A New Rulebook

The end of the Cold War accelerated a turn toward economic liberalization. Governments reduced barriers, privatized state enterprises, welcomed investment, and argued that markets would allocate resources more efficiently. Reagan, Thatcher, Deng, and Pinochet pursued different versions under very different political systems.

A New Geography of Work

Information technology helped knowledge economies grow in Finland, Japan, and the United States while firms placed manufacturing in lower-wage production zones in Vietnam, Bangladesh, Mexico, and Honduras. Globalization connected design, finance, assembly, shipping, and retail across borders.

Change Inside Continuity
