The Cahokia Council

Cahokia is the largest city north of Mexico — a thriving center of trade, ceremony, and political power near the confluence of the Mississippi and Missouri rivers. Thousands of people have migrated here, drawn by the city's wealth and influence. But growth has created tension. Food supplies are strained, surrounding communities resent Cahokia's dominance, and the great mound-building projects demand enormous labor. The council has been called to decide how Cahokia should respond.

Step 2

Central Dilemma

The paramount chief asks: How should Cahokia respond to the pressures of growth, labor demands, and tensions with neighboring communities?

Each option has benefits and consequences. There is no simple correct answer. Your recommendation must fit your role and use historical evidence.

Continue Mound Building and Ceremony

Invest in the great mound-building projects and ceremonial activities that demonstrate Cahokia's power and maintain spiritual legitimacy.

Prioritize Agricultural Expansion

Redirect labor from mound building to expanding farmland and food storage. Feed the city first, build monuments later.

Strengthen Trade Networks

Send emissaries to distant communities to expand exchange networks. Use prestige goods — copper, shells, pottery — to maintain alliances without coercion.

Negotiate with Neighboring Communities

Offer surrounding communities more autonomy and fairer exchange in return for continued food and labor. Build loyalty through generosity rather than demands.
