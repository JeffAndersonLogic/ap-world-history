Foundations Command Center

Five meetings. One launch sequence.

This unit is designed for a two-week Green/Silver block cycle: five class meetings per section before students enter Topic 1.1 and the AP World course content after 1200.

Purpose

Students do not need to memorize all of ancient history. They need the systems that explain why the world of 1200 looked the way it did.

Essential Question

How did the world before 1200 create the foundations for the global patterns we study in AP World: Modern?

Final Product

A one-page synthesis and SAQ-style response explaining how pre-1200 foundations shaped the AP World starting point.

Learning Focus

Learning Targets & Success Criteria

Learning Targets

  • I can explain why AP World begins around 1200 rather than at the beginning of world history.
  • I can describe how states and empires used tools of power before 1200.
  • I can compare major belief systems and explain how they shaped societies.
  • I can explain how trade networks connected Afro-Eurasia before 1200.
  • I can connect pre-1200 foundations to Unit 1 and the world after 1200.

Success Criteria

  • I can use terms such as agriculture, state, empire, bureaucracy, legitimacy, and hierarchy correctly.
  • I can identify at least three major belief systems and explain their social or political significance.
  • I can map major pre-1200 trade networks and describe what moved through them.
  • I can write an AP-style explanation using specific pre-1200 evidence.
